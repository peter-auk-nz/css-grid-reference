Gradients

Create smooth color transitions with linear and radial gradients

What are CSS Gradients?

Gradients are smooth transitions between two or more colors. They're treated as images in CSS, so they can be used anywhere an image can (backgrounds, borders, etc.). Gradients eliminate the need for image files, reducing page weight and improving load times.

Key Advantage: Infinitely scalable, lightweight, and easy to modify in code.

Linear Gradients

Creates a gradient along a straight line.

background: linear-gradient(to right, red, blue);

Direction: to right, to bottom, to top left, or degrees (45deg, 90deg)

Multiple colors: linear-gradient(red, yellow, green)

Example: Creates smooth transition from red to blue going left to right

Color Stops

Controls where colors start and end in the gradient.

background: linear-gradient(red 0%, yellow 50%, green 100%);

Without stops: Colors evenly distributed

With stops: Precise control over transition points

Example: Yellow appears exactly at the midpoint

Radial Gradients

Creates a gradient radiating from a center point.

background: radial-gradient(circle, red, blue);

Shape: circle or ellipse (default)

Size: closest-side, farthest-side, closest-corner, farthest-corner

Position: at top, at center, at 20% 80%

Repeating Gradients

Creates patterns by repeating gradients.

background: repeating-linear-gradient(45deg, red 0px, red 10px, blue 10px, blue 20px);

Linear: repeating-linear-gradient()

Radial: repeating-radial-gradient()

Use: Stripes, patterns, backgrounds

Conic Gradients

Creates a gradient rotating around a center point.

background: conic-gradient(red, yellow, green, blue, red);

Like a pie chart: Colors rotate around the center

Position: from angle at position

Use: Color wheels, pie charts, loading spinners

Gradient Transparency

Combine gradients with transparency for fade effects.

background: linear-gradient(to bottom, rgba(0,0,0,0), rgba(0,0,0,1));

Use: Fade to transparent, overlay effects, image masks

Multiple Gradients

Layer multiple gradients for complex effects.

background: linear-gradient(to right, transparent, white),
            linear-gradient(to bottom, blue, red);

Order matters: First gradient on top

Use: Complex backgrounds, overlays, artistic effects
